Info

STD is a sexually transmited disease caused by sexual contact which can cause infections. STD's can also be spread by skin to skin contact, sharing of used needles, and mixed infected body fluids. It also can be passed through breast feeding and childbirth.

Treatable STD's

-Chlamydia: is a very small parasitic bacterium. Can be spreaded through sexual contact and the medication is antibiotics.

-Gonorrhea: is caused by an infection with the bacteria Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It's spread through sexual contact with an infected person. This bacteria is treated with antibiotics such as ceftriaxone, azithromycin, and doxycycline.

-Trichomoniasis: is caused by parasite. To prevent it use condoms. The medication is antibiotics and the treatment involves both partners taking one large dose of a specific oral antibiotic.

-Syphilis: is caused during a sexual intercourse. Antibiotics like Penicillin are used as treatment. Both sexual partners should be treated.

Non-treatable STD's

-HIV/AIDS: AIDS start off as HIV, but if the immune system is too weak that's when it evolves to AIDS. HIV stands for Human immunodeficiency virus, which is a virus that attacks the immune system. AIDS stands for Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome. It is found in all the tissues of the body. This is spread through contaminated body fluids. There is no cure for aids but there is medication to help calm the symptoms.

-Hepatitis B: is a disease of inflammation in the liver. Hepatitis B is not curable, but there are antiviral drugs that can help.

-Herpes: is a virus affecting the skin usually in genital pain and sores. There is no cure for herpes, but there is medication available to treat the symptoms.

-HPV: is a viral infection caused by skin to skin contact. HPV stands for Human papillomavirus infection. There is no cure for HPV, but medication for symptoms is available.
